# Kosovo War (1999)

## Overview
NATO's intervention in Yugoslavia without explicit UN Security Council authorisation. A turning point for sovereignty, humanitarian intervention, and the R2P debate.

## Key Themes
- Humanitarian intervention without UNSC mandate
- Sovereignty vs human rights
- NATO's first out-of-area operation
- Post-Cold War intervention norms
- Legal vs legitimate intervention

## Related Theories
- [[Sovereignty]] — challenged by humanitarian intervention
- [[International Organizations]] — NATO acting without UN mandate
- [[International Law]] — questioned legality, but claimed legitimacy
- [[Peace and Conflict Studies]] — peace enforcement vs peacekeeping
- [[Liberalism]] — democratic peace, human rights trump sovereignty
- [[Realism]] — NATO acted from Western strategic interests

## Key Questions
1. Was Kosovo legal? Was it legitimate?
2. Does humanitarian intervention weaken or strengthen the sovereignty norm?
3. What precedent did Kosovo set for later interventions (Libya 2011, Iraq 2003)?
4. How does Kosovo challenge Westphalian sovereignty?
