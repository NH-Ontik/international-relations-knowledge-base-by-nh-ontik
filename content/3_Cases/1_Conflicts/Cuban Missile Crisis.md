# Cuban Missile Crisis (1962)

## Overview
The closest the Cold War came to nuclear war. Soviet deployment of nuclear missiles in Cuba triggered a US naval blockade and 13-day diplomatic standoff. The quintessential case study for crisis decision-making and deterrence theory.

## Key Themes
- Nuclear brinkmanship
- Crisis decision-making under pressure
- Deterrence success/failure
- Bureaucratic politics in foreign policy
- Communication and misperception

## Related Theories
- [[Foreign Policy Analysis]] — Graham Allison's *Essence of Decision* (3 models: Rational Actor, Organizational Process, Bureaucratic Politics)
- [[Deterrence]] — classic case of compellence vs deterrence
- [[Strategic Studies]] — crisis management, escalation control
- [[Security Dilemma]] — USSR saw Cuba as defensive, US as offensive
- [[Realism]] — structural pressure, anarchy

## Key Questions
1. Was the resolution due to diplomacy, coercion, or luck?
2. Does the crisis support rational actor or bureaucratic politics models?
3. What lessons does it offer for contemporary nuclear crises?
4. How did the crisis transform US-Soviet relations (Hotline, LTBT 1963)?
