# Israel–Palestine Conflict

## Overview
One of the longest unresolved conflicts in modern IR, central to postcolonial critique, realism, and peace studies. Tests every dimension of IR theory: territory, identity, sovereignty, international law, and conflict resolution.

## Key Themes
- Settler colonialism / postcolonial lens
- UN partition plan and recognition politics
- Occupation and international law (ICJ, Geneva Conventions)
- Two-state solution vs one-state reality
- US foreign policy toward Israel
- UNRWA and Palestinian refugees

## Related Theories
- [[Postcolonialism]] — Orientalist framing, colonial settlement, dehumanisation of Palestinians
- [[Realism]] — US strategic alliance with Israel, balance of power in Middle East
- [[International Law]] — occupation legality, ICJ advisory opinions
- [[Sovereignty]] — Palestinian statehood bid
- [[Peace and Conflict Studies]] — conflict transformation, track II diplomacy
- [[International Organizations]] — UN resolutions, UNRWA

## Key Questions
1. Why has the two-state solution failed?
2. How does postcolonial theory reframe the conflict?
3. What role does the US play as mediator/ally?
4. What are the conditions for a just peace?
5. How does the conflict shape Middle Eastern alliance patterns?
