# Rwanda Genocide (1994)

## Overview
Approximately 800,000 Tutsi and moderate Hutu killed in 100 days while the UN peacekeeping force (UNAMIR) failed to intervene. The defining failure of humanitarian intervention and R2P.

## Key Themes
- Genocide prevention failure
- UN peacekeeping limitations
- R2P (Responsibility to Protect) origins
- Colonial legacy (Hamitic thesis, Belgian ethnic classification)
- Postcolonial critique of intervention

## Related Theories
- [[International Organizations]] — UN failure, Chapter VI vs VII
- [[Sovereignty]] — clash with humanitarian intervention
- [[Peace and Conflict Studies]] — conflict prevention, early warning
- [[Postcolonialism]] — colonial roots of ethnic division, Western inaction prioritising state interests
- [[Feminism]] — gender-based violence during genocide (ICTY/ICTR precedents)
- [[International Law]] — ICTR established, genocide convention

## Key Questions
1. Why did the international community fail to intervene?
2. Was Rwanda a failure of will, capability, or mandate?
3. How did Rwanda shape the R2P doctrine?
4. What role did colonial legacies play in the genocide?
