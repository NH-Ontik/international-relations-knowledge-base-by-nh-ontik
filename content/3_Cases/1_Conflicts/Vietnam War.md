# Vietnam War (1955–1975)

## Overview
The quintessential case study of limited war, counterinsurgency failure, and the limits of superpower power. Central to strategic studies and foreign policy analysis.

## Key Themes
- Limited war theory
- Counterinsurgency failure
- Domino theory and containment
- Anti-war movement and public opinion
- Credibility trap
- Lessons for future interventions

## Related Theories
- [[Strategic Studies]] — limited war (Kissinger, Schelling), counterinsurgency doctrine
- [[Foreign Policy Analysis]] — cognitive biases (analogy to Munich), credibility obsession
- [[Realism]] — failure of geopolitical overreach
- [[Marxism]] — nationalist/communist revolution resisting imperialism
- [[Postcolonialism]] — French colonial legacy, nationalist resistance

## Key Questions
1. Why did the world's strongest military lose?
2. What role did the anti-war movement play?
3. How did Vietnam shape the "Vietnam syndrome" in US foreign policy?
4. What are the lessons for asymmetric warfare?
5. How do different IR theories explain the outcome?
