# Ukraine War (2022–present)

## Overview
Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine (Feb 2022–present), the largest conventional war in Europe since 1945. Tests nearly every IR theory and concept in the discipline.

## Key Themes
- Sovereignty violation vs R2P
- NATO expansion as security dilemma trigger
- Liberal order vs great power politics
- Nuclear escalation risk
- Economic sanctions as foreign policy tool
- Hybrid warfare

## Related Theories
- [[Realism]] — great power competition, sphere of influence, structural constraints
- [[Liberalism]] — failure of cooperative security, liberal order under threat
- [[Constructivism]] — identity (Russian vs Ukrainian), nationalism, narrative contestation
- [[Sovereignty]] — direct violation of territorial sovereignty
- [[International Law]] — UN Charter Art 2(4) violation, ICC arrest warrant for Putin
- [[Strategic Studies]] — conventional war, nuclear signalling, hybrid warfare
- [[Deterrence]] — NATO nuclear deterrence tested
- [[International Organizations]] — UN paralysis, NATO resurgence, EU candidate status for Ukraine
- [[Peace and Conflict Studies]] — peacemaking challenges, track diplomacy, conflict transformation

## Key Questions
1. Does the Ukraine war vindicate realism or liberalism? (Both claim it.)
2. What explains NATO's resilience and enlargement?
3. How does the war affect the Global South?
4. What are the conditions for a just peace?
5. Relevance of [[Kautilyan Realism]] — Russia as *Vijigishu*, Europe as *Mandal*?
