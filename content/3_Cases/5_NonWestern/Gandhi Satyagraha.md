# Gandhi / Satyagraha

## Overview
Mahatma Gandhi's nonviolent resistance (satyagraha) against British colonial rule in India. The foundational case for nonviolent conflict transformation and a key reference in [[Peace and Conflict Studies]].

## Key Themes
- Nonviolence as strategic method
- Civil disobedience and mass mobilisation
- Colonialism and decolonisation
- Truth-force (*satyagraha*) vs passive resistance conception
- Moral power vs material power

## Related Theories
- [[Peace and Conflict Studies]] — nonviolent conflict transformation
- [[Postcolonialism]] — anti-colonial strategy, agency of the colonised
- [[Strategic Studies]] — asymmetric strategy, moral jiu-jitsu

## Key Questions
1. Can nonviolence succeed against a determined adversary?
2. Is satyagraha a strategy or a moral philosophy?
3. How did satyagraha influence later movements (MLK, Mandela)?
4. What are the limits of nonviolence as a conflict strategy?
