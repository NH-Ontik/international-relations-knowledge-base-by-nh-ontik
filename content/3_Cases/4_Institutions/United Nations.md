# United Nations

## Overview
The foundational global institution. Central to liberal institutionalism, sovereignty debates, peace and security studies, and IO theory. Tests every major IR theory.

## Key Themes
- Collective security vs great power politics
- UNSC veto as realist constraint
- Peacekeeping evolution (Chapter VI → VII → R2P)
- UN reform debates
- Legitimacy vs effectiveness

## Related Theories
- [[International Organizations]] — structure, reform, effectiveness
- [[Liberalism]] — embodies liberal institutionalism
- [[Realism]] — UNSC P5 veto shows power politics
- [[Constructivism]] — UN as norm entrepreneur (R2P, human rights)
- [[Sovereignty]] — Article 2(4) prohibition on force vs sovereignty erosion
- [[Peace and Conflict Studies]] — peacekeeping, peacebuilding

## Key Cases
- [[Rwanda Genocide]] — peacekeeping failure
- [[Kosovo War]] — UNSC bypassed
- [[Ukraine War]] — UN paralysis
- [[Cold War]] — UN frozen by bipolarity

## Key Questions
1. Is the UN effective or just a forum?
2. Should the UNSC be reformed? How?
3. What explains the gap between UN ideals and performance?
4. Does the UN constrain power or legitimise it?
