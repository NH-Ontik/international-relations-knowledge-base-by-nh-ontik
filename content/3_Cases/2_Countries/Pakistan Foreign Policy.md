# Pakistan Foreign Policy

## Overview
Pakistan's foreign policy is fundamentally shaped by its rivalry with India, leading to a security-centric, alliance-seeking strategy. The primary comparative case to India in comparative foreign policy.

## Key Themes
- India-centric security paradigm
- Alliance-seeking behaviour (US Cold War ally, China all-weather partner)
- Nuclear deterrence against conventionally superior India
- Afghanistan policy (strategic depth, Taliban ties)
- Kashmir as core issue

## Related Theories
- [[Comparative Foreign Policy]] — direct comparison: same region as India but different foreign policy
- [[Security Dilemma]] — India-Pakistan competition as classic case
- [[Deterrence]] — nuclear deterrence at sub-conventional level (Kargil 1999)
- [[Strategic Studies]] — asymmetrical deterrence, proxy warfare

## Key Questions
1. Why has Pakistan sought external alliances while India has not?
2. Does nuclear deterrence work at the sub-conventional level?
3. How does the India rivalry shape every dimension of Pakistan's foreign policy?
4. What explains the shift from US ally to China client?
