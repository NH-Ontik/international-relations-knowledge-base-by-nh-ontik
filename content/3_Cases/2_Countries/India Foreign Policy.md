# India Foreign Policy

## Overview
India's foreign policy is uniquely shaped by non-alignment (Nehru), Kautilyan strategic tradition, and contemporary balancing. Central to comparative foreign policy and non-Western IR.

## Key Themes
- Non-Alignment Movement origins
- Strategic autonomy doctrine
- Kautilyan *Mandal* framework (neighbourhood as concentric circles)
- *Asana* (posture) — non-alignment as strategic flexibility
- Rising great power, but non-Western identity
- Pakistan-China competition as structural driver

## Related Theories
- [[Kautilyan Realism]] — direct application: *Saptanga* (state elements), *Shadgunya* (six measures)
- [[Comparative Foreign Policy]] — comparison with Pakistan (same region, different policies)
- [[International Organizations]] — SCO, BRICS, G4 (UNSC reform bid)
- [[Strategic Studies]] — nuclear posture (No First Use), China border disputes
- [[Postcolonialism]] — anti-colonial identity, Global South leadership

## Key Cases
- [[Bangladesh]] neighbour relations
- India-Pakistan rivalry (see [[Falklands War]] for FPA comparison)
- India-US nuclear deal (2008)
- India-China border standoffs

## Key Questions
1. Is non-alignment still relevant in a bipolarising world?
2. How does Kautilyan theory explain India's foreign policy?
3. Can India balance between US and Russia?
4. What is India's role in the Global South?
