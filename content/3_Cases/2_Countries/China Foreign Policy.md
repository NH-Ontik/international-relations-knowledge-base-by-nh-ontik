# China Foreign Policy

## Overview
China's rise is the defining structural change of the 21st century international system. Central case for US-China rivalry, BRICS, revisionist vs status quo debate, and non-Western IR theory.

## Key Themes
- Peaceful Rise / Peaceful Development doctrine
- Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) as alternative development model
- South China Sea and territorial assertiveness
- Wolf Warrior diplomacy
- Status quo vs revisionist power debate
- Constructivist: identity as developing country / victim of colonialism

## Related Concepts
- [[Hegemony]] — will China challenge or replace?
- [[Balance of Power]] — US-China rivalry
- [[Realism]] — China as revisionist power (Mearsheimer)
- [[Liberalism]] — engagement via institutions (WTO, BRI)
- [[International Development]] — BRI as alternative to Western model
- [[Postcolonialism]] — Century of Humiliation narrative

## Key Questions
1. Is China a status quo or revisionist power?
2. Can the US and China avoid a Thucydides Trap?
3. Does BRI represent a new development paradigm?
4. How does China's history shape its IR behaviour?
5. Is China challenging or joining the liberal order?
