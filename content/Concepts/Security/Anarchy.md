# Anarchy

## Definition
The absence of a central authority or sovereign above states in the international system. Anarchy does not mean chaos — it means there is no world government. This is the foundational assumption from which most IR theories begin (especially [[Realism]] and [[Neorealism]]).

## Key Debate
- **Realists** (Waltz): Anarchy necessitates self-help and generates security competition. States must provide for their own security because no one will do it for them.
- **Liberals** (Keohane): Anarchy does not preclude cooperation. Institutions can mitigate its effects.
- **Constructivists** (Wendt): "Anarchy is what states make of it." The meaning of anarchy depends on whether states see each other as enemies, rivals, or friends.
- **English School** (Bull): An anarchical *society* exists — states share norms and institutions even without a central authority.

## Origins
- Classical: Hobbes's state of nature as "war of all against all"
- IR: Waltz's *Theory of International Politics* (1979) — anarchy as ordering principle of international politics
- Waltz argues: domestic politics is hierarchic; international politics is anarchic
