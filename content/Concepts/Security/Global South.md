# Global South

A term broadly referring to developing, less-industrialised, and formerly colonised countries, primarily in Africa, Asia, Latin America, and Oceania. It functions as both a geographical descriptor and a political-economic category that captures the structural inequalities of the world system. Originating from Cold War categories of "Third World" and "developing countries," the term Global South emphasises shared experiences of colonial exploitation, economic dependency, and marginalisation in global governance. Key institutional expressions include the [[Non-Aligned Movement]] (NAM, 1961), the G77, and [[BRICS]]. Critics note the term risks flattening vast differences between countries such as India and Haiti, while supporters argue it captures a real power asymmetry in the international order. Central to [[Postcolonialism|postcolonial]] and [[Marxism|Marxist]] critiques of mainstream IR.
