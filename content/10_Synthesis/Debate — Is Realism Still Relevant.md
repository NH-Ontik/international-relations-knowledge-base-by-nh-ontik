# Debate — Is Realism Still Relevant?

The end of the Cold War appeared to falsify realism; great power competition revived it.

## PRO — Mearsheimer, Walt, Friedberg

**Great power competition returns** (Mearsheimer 2001, Friedberg 2000): US-China rivalry confirms structural realism's prediction that rising powers challenge hegemonic states. Anarchy persists — the international system has no overarching authority. The [[Thucydides Trap]] (Allison 2017) frames US-China tension as structural, not ideological.

**Balance of power logic holds** (Walt 2018): States respond to threats by balancing — NATO's post-2014 reinforcement, the [[AUKUS]] pact, and Japan's remilitarization all demonstrate balancing behavior. [[Bipolarity]] returns as US-China competition structures global politics.

**Deterrence works** (Mearsheimer 2014): Nuclear deterrence prevents great power war. The logic of nuclear stability — second-strike capability, mutually assured destruction — remains sound. [[Russia 2022]] nuclear signaling demonstrates deterrence in practice.

## CON — Nye, Ikenberry, Keohane

**Interdependence constrains** (Nye 2011): Economic globalization creates mutual dependence that raises the costs of conflict. US-China economic integration makes war materially irrational despite political tensions. [[Complex interdependence]] (Keohane & Nye 1977) persists.

**Institutions matter** (Ikenberry 2011): The liberal international order — [[UN]], [[WTO]], [[NATO]] — constrains state behavior through rules, norms, and institutional path dependence. Even adversaries cooperate through institutions (US-Russia arms control, UN peacekeeping).

**Nuclear taboo holds** (Tannenwald 2007): The norm against nuclear use — not just deterrence — prevents nuclear war. The taboo is socially constructed, not structurally determined.

## SYNTHESIS

Realism explains **competition** well but **cooperation** poorly. Structural realism illuminates anarchy, balancing, and power politics. Liberal institutionalism explains how institutions, interdependence, and norms generate cooperation within anarchy. Not either/or but **scope conditions**: realism dominates in high-threat, low-interaction contexts; liberalism dominates in low-threat, high-interaction contexts.
