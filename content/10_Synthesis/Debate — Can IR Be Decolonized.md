# Debate — Can IR Be Decolonized?

The question of whether IR theory is inherently Western has become one of the discipline's most contested debates.

## PRO — Acharya, Shilliam, Aboubacar

**IR is Eurocentric by origin** (Acharya 2014): The discipline emerged from European great-power politics and colonial administration. [[Waltz 1979]], [[Morgenthau 1948]], and [[Mearsheimer 2001]] all reflect Western historical experience. Non-Western states appear as "cases" to test Western theories, not as sources of theory.

**Non-Western traditions exist** (Shilliam 2010): Japanese strategic thought ([[Sun Tzu]], [[Tokugawa]]), Islamic jurisprudence on war and peace ([[jus ad bellum]]), African ubuntu philosophy, and Chinese tianxia all offer rich strategic traditions that preceded or parallel Western IR.

**"Global IR" needed** (Acharya 2014): IR must move beyond Western-centric universalism to genuine "worlded" theorizing. This means incorporating non-Western experiences as sources of theory, not just data for testing Western propositions.

## CON — Schmidt, Vasquez, Jackson

**IR emerged from specific historical experience** (Schmidt 2008): The discipline's Eurocentric origins do not invalidate its theoretical insights. Physics emerged in Europe but is universal; IR theories can be universally applicable despite their origins.

**Universal theories need not be Western** (Vasquez 2018): Theories like [[balance of power]], [[security dilemma]], and [[interdependence]] describe universal dynamics. Their Western origin is irrelevant to their explanatory power.

**Methodological cosmopolitanism** (Jackson 2011): Existing frameworks can accommodate non-Western perspectives without wholesale disciplinary revolution. [[English School]] already incorporates international society as a normative, not just Western, concept.

## SYNTHESIS

Decolonization is necessary but insufficient. "Adding" non-Western cases to Western theories is tokenism. Genuine pluralism requires: (1) recognizing non-Western knowledge systems as theory, not just data; (2) acknowledging the power dynamics embedded in knowledge production; (3) building methodological frameworks that accommodate diverse epistemologies. [[Acharya 2014]] "Global IR" is a beginning, not an end.
