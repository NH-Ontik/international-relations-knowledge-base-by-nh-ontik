# Feminism — Syria Conflict

Feminist IR reveals dimensions of the Syrian conflict invisible to state-centric theories.

## Gendered Impacts of Displacement

Syria's 13+ million displaced persons (6.8 million external refugees, 6.9 million internally displaced) experience displacement gendered differently. Women and girls face heightened risks of sexual violence in refugee camps and during flight. [[Enloe 2014]] argues that "womenandchildren" as a category obscures the agency and specific vulnerabilities of each. In Turkey, Lebanon, and Jordan, Syrian refugee women face restricted mobility, early marriage, and economic marginalization — gender intersects with refugee status to compound disadvantage.

## Sexual Violence as Weapon of War

Documented sexual violence in Syria — by Assad regime forces, ISIS, and opposition groups — represents what [[Hudson 2012]] calls the "feminization of insecurity." systematic rape in detention centers (documented by the UN Commission of Inquiry) functions as state terror. ISIS's yazidi enslavement made sexual violence institutional policy. Feminist analysis rejects the framing of sexual violence as "collateral damage" — it is a deliberate strategy of war.

## Exclusion from Peace Processes

The Geneva peace talks (2012-2018) systematically excluded women. [[UNSCR 1325]] (2000) called for women's participation in peace processes, yet Syrian women's groups were marginalized. The Constitutional Committee (2019) included minimal female representation. [[Paffenholz 2016]] demonstrates that peace agreements with women's participation are 35% more likely to last 15+ years.

## Militarized Masculinity

[[Cohn 2013]] analyzes how militarized masculinity drives conflict escalation. In Syria, masculine honor codes intersect with sectarian identity and nationalist rhetoric. Male combatants on all sides perform hypermasculinity through violence. The intersectional lens ([[Crenshaw 1991]]) reveals how gender intersects with sect (Sunni, Alawi, Kurdish), class, and refugee status to produce differentiated vulnerabilities.
