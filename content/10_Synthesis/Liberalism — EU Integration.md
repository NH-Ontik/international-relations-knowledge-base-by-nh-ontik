# Liberalism — EU Integration

The European Union is the paradigmatic case for liberal international relations theory.

## Institutional Cooperation

[[Keohane 1984]] identified the EU (then EC) as the exemplar of how institutions reduce transaction costs, provide information, and create focal points for cooperation. The EU demonstrates that repeated interaction under [[anarchy]] can generate stable cooperation — states comply with rules not because they are forced but because institutions lower the costs of coordination and create expectations of reciprocity.

## Functional Spillover

[[Haas 1958]] and [[Lindberg 1963]] proposed that integration in one technical sector (coal and steel) creates pressures for integration in adjacent sectors — "spillover." Integration in trade led to monetary coordination (Eurozone), which created pressures for fiscal coordination. The single market required common regulatory standards, which generated demands for political harmonization. This logic drove EU expansion from 6 to 27 members.

## Democratic Peace Within the EU

The EU embodies [[democratic peace theory]] at the regional level: member states have not fought each other since 1945. The EU's institutional design — shared sovereignty, veto points, parliamentary oversight — constrains conflict and socializes leaders into cooperative norms. [[Moravcsik 1998]] emphasizes "liberal intergovernmentalism": member states rationally designed institutions to lock in cooperative gains.

## Where Liberalism Fails

- [[Brexit]]: Identity politics and sovereignty concerns overrode economic interests — liberalism struggles when identity trumps material calculations
- [[Eurozone crisis]]: Institutional design flaws (monetary union without fiscal union) revealed limits of functional spillover logic
- [[Democratic deficit]]: EU institutions are perceived as distant and unaccountable, undermining liberal legitimacy claims

The EU shows liberalism's strengths in explaining institutional cooperation and its weaknesses when identity, domestic politics, and inequality enter the picture.
