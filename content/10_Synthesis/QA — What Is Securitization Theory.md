# QA — What Is Securitization Theory?

**Q: What is securitization theory and how does it work?**

**A:** Securitization theory, developed by the **Copenhagen School** ([[Buzan]], [[Wæver]], de Wilde 1998), analyzes how issues are moved from normal politics to the realm of emergency action through speech acts.

**The process**: Securitization is a **speech act** — saying "this is an existential threat" and having it accepted by an audience. It requires three elements: (1) A **securitizing actor** — a leader, institution, or group that claims an issue threatens a referent object. (2) A **referent object** — what is claimed to be existentially threatened: the state, national identity, the environment, the economy, a cultural group. (3) An **audience** — the public, elites, or international community whose acceptance legitimizes emergency measures.

**Success conditions**: Securitization succeeds when the securitizing actor convinces the audience that normal political procedures are insufficient to address the existential threat. It moves the issue beyond the bounds of legitimate contestation — once securitized, dissent becomes illegitimate ("you don't understand the threat").

**Examples**: [[Terrorism]] was securitized post-9/11 — normal law enforcement became insufficient; extraordinary measures (Guantánamo, surveillance, torture) became legitimate. [[Migration]] was securitized in Europe — economic migration became a threat to national identity. [[Climate change]] was securitized in Pacific Islands — rising sea levels became an existential threat to state survival.

**Desecuritization**: The Copenhagen School's normative goal is **desecuritization** — returning securitized issues to normal political debate. Not every security issue requires emergency measures; most are better handled through ordinary politics.

**Critique**: [[Hansen 2000]] argues securitization theory underemphasizes the role of emotions and affect. [[Salter 2008]] critiques its focus on elite speech acts, neglecting material practices. The theory can be **abused** — [[Iraq WMD]] claims demonstrate how securitization can manufacture consent for war.
