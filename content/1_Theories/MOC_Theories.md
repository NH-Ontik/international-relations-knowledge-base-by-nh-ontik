# Theories — Map of Content

## Classical IR Theories

| Theory | Core Argument | Key Thinkers |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| [[Realism]] | States seek power and security in an anarchic international system | Morgenthau, Waltz, Mearsheimer |
| [[Neorealism]] | System structure (polarity) determines state behaviour, not human nature | Waltz |
| [[Liberalism]] | Cooperation, institutions, and democracy enable peace and mutual gain | Kant, Keohane, Doyle |
| [[Constructivism]] | Ideas, norms, identities, and social interaction shape state interests | Wendt, Finnemore, Katzenstein |
| [[Marxism]] | Capitalism, class conflict, and economic structures drive world politics | Marx, Lenin, Wallerstein, Cox |

## Critical Theories

| Theory | Core Argument | Key Thinkers |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| [[Feminism]] | Gender hierarchy and patriarchal structures shape all dimensions of IR | Tickner, Enloe, Sylvester, Cohn |
| [[Postcolonialism]] | Colonial legacies and coloniality of power structure contemporary world politics | Said, Spivak, Fanon, Chakrabarty, Grovogui |
| [[Critical Theory]] | Emancipation from domination through reflexive critique of existing structures | Horkheimer, Adorno, Cox, Linklater |
| [[Eco-Critical IR]] | Ecological crisis demands fundamental rethinking of IR's state-centrism and anthropocentrism | — |

## Specialist Frameworks

| Framework | Core Question | Key Thinkers |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| [[English School]] | Do states form an international society with shared rules and norms? | Bull, Wight, Buzan, Vincent |
| [[International Political Economy]] | How do politics and economics interact to shape global order? | Strange, Gilpin, Cox, Keohane |
| [[Rational Choice and Game Theory]] | What do rational actors choose under strategic interaction? | Downs, Axelrod, Keohane, Fearon |

## Non-Western Theories

| Theory | Core Argument | Key Thinkers |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| [[Kautilyan Realism]] | Ancient Indian statecraft offers a non-Western realist framework emphasizing pragmatism and state survival | Kautilya (Chanakya) |
| [[Islamic Political Thought in IR]] | Islamic political concepts — umma, jihad, shura — challenge secular Western IR categories | Ibn Khaldun, Qutb, Iqbal, Maududi, Abou El Fadl |
| [[Chinese Strategic Thought]] | Relational ontology and harmony-seeking offer an alternative to anarchy-centric Western IR | Sun Tzu, Confucius, Han Fei, Mao, Qin Yaqing |
| [[African Political Philosophy in IR]] | Ubuntu communalism and Pan-Africanism challenge Western individualism and state-centrism | Tutu, Ramose, Nkrumah, Fanon, Nyerere |
| [[Latin American Dependency Theory]] | Core-periphery exploitation structures global economic inequality | Prebisch, Frank, Cardoso, Dos Santos, Furtado |

## Emerging Theories

| Theory | Core Argument | Key Thinkers |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| [[Practice Theory]] | IR is constituted through practitioners' everyday routines, not just ideas or structures | Bourdieu, Adler & Pouliot, Weldes |
| [[Ontological Security Theory]] | States seek identity continuity and narrative coherence, not just physical security | Goffman, Mitzen, Steele |
| [[Assemblage Theory and ANT]] | International politics emerges from heterogeneous networks of human and non-human actants | DeLuze & Guattari, Latour, Callon |
| [[Quantum IR]] | Quantum concepts — entanglement, complementarity, indeterminacy — challenge classical IR assumptions | Smith, Law, Manners |
| [[Disability Studies in IR]] | Disability is a political category that intersects with war, development, and human rights | — |
