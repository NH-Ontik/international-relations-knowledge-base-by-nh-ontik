# Citation Guide for IR

## Why Citation Matters in IR

Citation is not merely a formatting requirement; it is an epistemic practice. In IR, where claims about power, history, and legitimacy carry political weight, proper citation:
- Distinguishes your argument from existing scholarship
- Allows readers to verify claims and trace intellectual lineage
- Credits prior work and avoids plagiarism
- Demonstrates engagement with the literature (especially for examiners)

## APA 7th Edition

APA is the most common citation style in political science and IR.

### In-Text Citation
- Single author: (Morgenthau, 1948)
- Two authors: (Keohane & Nye, 1977)
- Three or more: (Waltz et al., 1989)
- Direct quote: (Morgenthau, 1948, p. 4)
- Multiple works: (Carr, 1939; Morgenthau, 1948)

### Reference List Format

**Journal Article:**
Wendt, A. (1992). Anarchy is what states make of it: The social construction of power politics. *International Organization*, *46*(2), 391–425. https://doi.org/10.1017/S0020818300001505

**Book:**
Waltz, K. (1979). *Theory of international politics*. McGraw-Hill.

**Edited Book Chapter:**
Keohane, R. O. (1986). Realism, neorealism and the study of world politics. In R. O. Keohane (Ed.), *Neorealism and its critics* (pp. 1–30). Columbia University Press.

**Website:**
Cox, R. W. (1981). Social forces, states and world orders: Beyond international relations theory. *Millennium: Journal of International Studies*, *10*(2), 126–155. Retrieved January 15, 2026, from https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/03058297810100020501

### IR-Specific: Citing Treaties and UN Documents

**Treaty:**
Treaty of Westphalia. (1648). *Peace of Westphalia*. Osnabrück/Münster.

**UN Security Council Resolution:**
United Nations Security Council. (2022). *Resolution 2623 (2022)* (S/RES/2623). https://undocs.org/S/RES/2623(2022)

**UN General Assembly Resolution:**
United Nations General Assembly. (2024). *Resolution 78/265* (A/RES/78/265). https://undocs.org/A/RES/78/265

**Policy Brief:**
International Crisis Group. (2023). *The state of African peace and security* (Africa Report No. 315). https://www.crisisgroup.org/africa

## Chicago/Turabian

Chicago is used in history-heavy IR scholarship. Two systems: Notes-Bibliography and Author-Date.

### Notes-Bibliography System (Footnotes)

**First reference to a source:**
¹ E. H. Carr, *The Twenty Years' Crisis, 1919–1939: An Introduction to the Study of International Relations* (London: Macmillan, 1939), 15.

**Subsequent references (shortened):**
² Carr, *Twenty Years' Crisis*, 103.

**Ibid. (same source as immediately preceding):**
³ Ibid., 25.

**Bibliography entry:**
Carr, E. H. *The Twenty Years' Crisis, 1919–1939: An Introduction to the Study of International Relations*. London: Macmillan, 1939.

### When to Use Chicago Over APA
- History-heavy essays, especially in diplomatic and political history
- When primary sources (speeches, archives, memoirs) are heavily used
- When footnotes allow richer commentary than parenthetical citations

## Harvard

Common in UK/Commonwealth IR programmes. Similar to APA with differences.

### In-Text Citation
- (Morgenthau 1948) — no comma between author and year
- (Keohane and Nye 1977) — "and" instead of "&"
- (Waltz et al. 1989) — "et al." for three or more

### Reference List Format
Morgenthau, H. (1948) *Politics among nations*. New York: Knopf.

Wendt, A. (1992) 'Anarchy is what states make of it: The social construction of power politics', *International Organization*, 46(2), pp. 391–425.

## IR-Specific Citation Challenges

### UN Documents (UN Doc Symbol System)
- **S/RES/[number]** — Security Council Resolutions
- **A/RES/[number]** — General Assembly Resolutions
- **S/[year]/[number]** — Security Council documents
- **A/[session]/[number]** — General Assembly documents
- **E/AC.51/[number]** — Economic and Social Council documents
- Always include the document symbol, not just a URL (URLs may change)

### Think Tank Reports
Cite as you would a policy brief:
- International Crisis Group. (2023). *Title* (Report No. XX). City: ICG.
- RAND Corporation. (2022). *Title* (Research Report). Santa Monica: RAND.
- Include the report number and URL if available

### Declassified Documents
- US National Security Archive: cite the archive and declassification date
- Example: National Security Council. (1961). *NSC 5602/1* [Declassified January 2004]. George Washington University.

### Interviews
- (Author's pseudonym or initials, personal communication, Date)
- For published interviews: cite the publication
- In qualitative IR research, interview methodology must be described in the methods section

### Datasets
- Cite the dataset, not the underlying data: V-Dem Project. (2024). *V-Dem Dataset v14*. https://www.v-dem.net/
- Include version number and access date if data is updated frequently

### Maps and GIS Data
- Cite the source and date of the data: Natural Earth. (2024). *1:110m Cultural Vectors*. https://www.naturalearthdata.com/

## Zotero Workflow for IR

### Setting Up Zotero
1. Install Zotero desktop application and browser connector
2. Install the Word/LibreOffice citation plugin
3. Create a "IR Research" collection
4. Install IR-relevant styles: APA 7th, Chicago, Harvard

### Best Practices
- **Group libraries** for collaborative projects (e.g., seminar groups, co-authored papers)
- **Tags** by theory (e.g., realism, liberalism), region, method, and topic
- **Notes** — Take reading notes within Zotero; link to page numbers in the PDF
- **Labels** — Use Zotero's "extra" field for UN document symbols, case study codes
- **Automatic import** — Use the browser connector to capture journal articles, reports, and websites
- **PDF management** — Attach PDFs to items; use Zotero's built-in reader for annotation
- **Cite While You Write (CWYW)** — Insert citations directly in Word/LibreOffice from your library
- **BibTeX export** — For LaTeX users, Zotero exports to BibTeX format

### Common Zotero Workflow
1. Find source → Browser connector saves metadata and PDF
2. Add tags and notes → Organise for later retrieval
3. Insert citation in Word → Select from library → Zotero formats automatically
4. Generate bibliography → Zotero produces the full reference list

## Common Mistakes

### Citing Wikipedia
- Wikipedia is a tertiary source, not a primary or secondary source
- If Wikipedia is the only source, the research is inadequate
- Use Wikipedia to find primary/secondary sources, then cite those

### Circular Referencing
- Citing a source that itself cites the original without the original being consulted
- Always trace claims back to the primary source
- Example: If Source B cites Source A for a claim, cite Source A directly

### Over-Reliance on Secondary Sources
- IR essays should engage with primary sources: treaties, speeches, official documents, data
- A well-researched essay cites both scholarly analysis AND the evidence it analyses
- Example: An essay on the Iraq War should cite both scholarly works AND UN resolutions, speeches, policy documents

### Not Citing Primary Sources
- Treaties, UN resolutions, government white papers, speeches, and datasets are primary sources
- They must be cited even when paraphrased
- Example: A claim about NATO's Article 5 should cite the NATO Treaty (1949), Article 5

### Inconsistent Citation Style
- Mixing APA and Chicago within one essay is a common error
- Choose one style and apply it consistently throughout
- Check your department's or journal's requirements
