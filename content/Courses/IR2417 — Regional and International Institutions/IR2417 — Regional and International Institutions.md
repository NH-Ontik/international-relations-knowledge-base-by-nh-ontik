## Core Concepts

### 1. What is an International Organization?
- **Intergovernmental Organization (IGO)**: State members, created by treaty (e.g., UN, EU, NATO)
- **Non-Governmental Organization (NGO)**: Non-state members, civil society (e.g., Amnesty, MSF, BRAC)
- **Supranational Organization**: Authority above states (e.g., EU law)

### 2. [[International Organizations|IR Theories of International Organizations]]

| Theory | View of IOs | Who IOs Serve | IO Autonomy |
|--------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| [[Realism]] | Instruments of state power | Powerful states; reflect power distribution | Minimal — states control IOs |
| [[Neorealism|Neoliberal Institutionalism]] | Facilitate cooperation | All states (by reducing transaction costs) | Moderate |
| [[Liberalism]] | Actors in their own right | Multiple actors, [[Democratic Peace Theory|democratic peace]], norms | Significant |
| [[Constructivism]] | Norm-setters, identity-shapers | International community; diffuse norms | High |
| [[English School]] | Key component of [[International Society\|international society]] | Create and maintain order | Moderate |
| **Functionalism** | Technical cooperation areas | Meet shared needs through cooperation | Context-dependent |
| **Neo-functionalism** | "Spillover" effect | Expand from technical → political integration | Growing |
| **Intergovernmentalism** | States remain in control | Preserve [[Sovereignty]] | Minimal |
| [[Critical Theory|Marxism/Critical]] | Legitimize capitalist order | Global elite, maintain inequality | Ideological |

### 3. [[United Nations|The United Nations System]]

**Principal Organs:**
| Organ | Composition | Function |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **General Assembly** | All member states (193) | Deliberative, recommendations, budget |
| **Security Council** | 5 permanent + 10 elective (2-year) | International peace and security; binding resolutions |
| **Secretariat** | Secretary-General + staff | Administration, implementation |
| [[International Law\|International Court of Justice]] | 15 judges | Legal disputes, advisory opinions |
| **Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)** | 54 members | Coordination of economic/social work |
| **Trusteeship Council** | Currently inactive | Former colonial territories |

**UN Reform Debate:**
- SC permanent members reflect 1945, not 2026
- Proposals: G4 (India, Japan, Germany, Brazil) for permanent seats; [[African Union]] representation
- Veto power criticized
- Budget and effectiveness concerns

### 4. Regional Organizations

| Organization | Type | Key Features |
|------------|------|-------------|
| [[European Union|European Union (EU)]] | Deepest integration | Single market, Euro, free movement, supranational law, European Parliament |
| [[ASEAN]] | Consensus-based | ASEAN Way, non-interference, ASEAN Economic Community, ASEAN + 3 |

### 5. Theories of Regional Integration

**Functionalism (Mitrany):**
- Start with technical cooperation (transport, postal, energy)
- Cooperation in one area creates habit of cooperation

**Neo-Functionalism (Haas):**
- "Spillover" effect: integration in one sector forces integration in others
- Supranational institutions (e.g., European Commission) drive the process

**Liberal Intergovernmentalism (Moravcsik):**
- Three stages: (1) domestic preference formation, (2) interstate bargaining, (3) institutional choice
- States remain in control; integration serves national interests

**Realism and Regionalism:**
- Regional institutions reflect power distribution
- Great powers use regional institutions to advance interests
- See: [[NATO]] as US tool, [[SCO]] as China-Russia vehicle

### 6. [[Bretton Woods Institutions]]

| Institution | Purpose | Key Feature |
|-------------|----------|------------|
| [[International Monetary Fund\|IMF]] (1944) | Financial stability, lender of last resort | Conditional loans, surveillance |
| [[World Bank]] (1944) | Development finance, infrastructure | IBRD, IDA for poor countries |
| [[World Trade Organization\|GATT/WTO]] (1947/1995) | Trade liberalization | Rules-based trade, dispute resolution |

**Critique of Bretton Woods:**
- Voting power weighted by economic size (US/Europe dominance)
- Structural adjustment policies (SAPs) in 1980s
- IMF conditionality criticized for harming the poor

### 7. Key Concepts
- [[Collective Security]] — "One for all, all for one"; response to aggression
- **Peacekeeping** — UN operations with consent, impartiality, non-use of force
- [[NATO|Collective Defence]] — NATO Article 5; "against" defined threat
- [[Humanitarian Intervention]] / [[Responsibility to Protect\|R2P]] — R2P (2005 World Summit)
- [[Global Governance]] — Not world government; regulation of transboundary issues
- [[International Regimes\|Regime Theory]] — Norms, principles, and rules that govern behaviour in issue-areas

### 8. Contemporary Challenges
- **Multilateralism under pressure** — Rising nationalism, unilateralism
- [[United Nations|UN Security Council]] paralysis — Veto use, political gridlock
- [[European Union|EU crises]] — Brexit, migration, democratic backsliding
- [[ASEAN]] effectiveness — Myanmar crisis strained ASEAN Way
- **Rising powers and institutions** — BRICS, [[SCO|New Development Bank]], AIIB
- **Global health governance** — [[Bretton Woods Institutions|WHO]] reform after COVID-19
