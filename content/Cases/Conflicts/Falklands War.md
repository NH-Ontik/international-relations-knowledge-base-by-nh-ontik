# Falklands War (1982)

## Overview
Argentina's military junta invaded the British Falkland Islands; Britain dispatched a naval task force and retook them after 10 weeks. A textbook case study for FPA and deterrence.

## Key Themes
- Diversionary war theory (regime survival)
- Misperception and signalling
- Deterrence failure
- Nationalism and public opinion
- Naval power projection

## Related Theories
- [[Foreign Policy Analysis]] — Argentina's decision to invade (Galtieri's gamble), UK's decision to respond (Thatcher)
- [[Deterrence]] — miscalculation: Argentina did not believe UK would respond
- [[Strategic Studies]] — amphibious operations, expeditionary warfare
- [[Realism]] — sovereignty, territorial integrity
- [[International Law]] — self-determination vs territorial integrity

## Key Questions
1. Why did Argentina misjudge British response?
2. Was the war a classic diversionary war?
3. How did the outcome affect both regimes (Thatcher's popularity, junta's collapse)?
4. Is the Falklands model of expeditionary warfare repeatable?
5. How does the case illustrate miscalculation in international politics?
