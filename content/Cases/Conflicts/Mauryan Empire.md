# Mauryan Empire (c. 322–185 BCE)

## Overview
The historical context for [[Kautilyan Realism]]. Chandragupta Maurya unified the Indian subcontinent under Kautilya's guidance using the doctrines of the Arthashastra. The foundational non-Western case study for statecraft, alliance, and strategic theory.

## Key Themes
- *Saptanga* (seven elements of state) in practice
- *Mandal* (circle of states) as geopolitical reality
- *Shadgunya* (six measures of foreign policy) in action
- *Kuta-yuddha* (covert/silent war)
- Expansion, consolidation, and administration

## Related Concepts
- [[Kautilyan Realism]] — theory derived from this historical case
- [[Strategic Studies]] — non-Western strategic thought
- [[Balance of Power]] — mandala system of allies, enemies, neutrals

## Key Questions
1. How did Kautilya's strategic advice shape the Mauryan rise?
2. Can Kautilyan theory travel from ancient India to modern IR?
3. What are the ethical implications of Kautilya's *Upayas* (methods)?
4. How does Mauryan expansion compare to other imperial cases (Rome, Qin)?
