# Iraq War / Gulf Conflicts (1991, 2003)

## Overview
Two critical US decision points: the 1991 decision NOT to invade Iraq after liberating Kuwait, and the 2003 decision TO invade Iraq. Central case study for Foreign Policy Analysis theories.

## 1991 — Gulf War
- Saddam invades Kuwait (Aug 1990)
- US-led coalition liberates Kuwait but stops at Baghdad
- **FPA case:** Rational choice model — George H.W. Bush's cost-benefit calculus
- Realist restraint: no occupation, UN coalition mandate

## 2003 — Iraq War
- US-led invasion based on WMD intelligence (none found)
- **FPA case:** Groupthink (Janis) — bureaucratic politics, flawed intelligence processes
- Liberal critique: preemptive war violated UN Charter
- Neoconservative agenda, democracy promotion narrative

## Related Theories
- [[Foreign Policy Analysis]] — groupthink, rational actor, organizational process models
- [[Realism]] — offensive vs defensive, Mearsheimer's prediction of failure
- [[Unipolarity]] — US exercised unconstrained power
- [[International Law]] — legality of preemptive self-defence
- [[Balance of Power]] — Iraq as regional balancer against Iran destroyed

## Key Questions
1. Why did the US invade in 2003 but not 1991?
2. Was the Iraq war a failure of intelligence or a failure of theory?
3. Does Iraq demonstrate the limits of US hegemony?
4. How does groupthink explain foreign policy disasters?
