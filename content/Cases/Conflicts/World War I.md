# World War I (1914–1918)

## Overview
The originating puzzle of modern IR discipline. Why did a relatively minor assassination trigger a continental war? The question that realism, liberalism, Marxism all claim to explain.

## Key Themes
- Alliance systems and security dilemma
- Mobilisation cascades and loss of control
- Offence-defence balance theory
- Liberal failure (globalisation did not prevent war)
- End of empires → new state system

## Related Theories
- [[Realism]] — structural anarchy, balance of power failure, security dilemma
- [[Liberalism]] — disproved liberal optimism (Norman Angell's *The Great Illusion*)
- [[Marxism]] — interimperialist rivalry, Lenin's theory of imperialism
- [[Security Dilemma]] — mobilisation as defensive perceived as offensive
- [[Balance of Power]] — why the system failed to prevent war
- [[Alliances]] — rigid alliance commitments as cause

## Key Questions
1. Was WWI inevitable given the structural conditions of 1914?
2. Which IR theory best explains the outbreak?
3. Did the war result from human error or structural forces?
4. How did WWI shape the development of IR as a discipline?
