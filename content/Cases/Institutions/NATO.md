# NATO

## Overview
The world's most powerful military alliance. A central case for alliance theory, institutional resilience, and the debate over whether institutions constrain or enable state power.

## Key Themes
- Collective defence (Article 5)
- Out-of-area operations (Kosovo, Afghanistan)
- NATO expansion and the Russia relationship
- NATO as liberal order vs realist alliance
- Burden-sharing debates
- Institutional adaptation post-Cold War

## Related Theories
- [[International Organizations]] — institutional resilience
- [[Liberalism]] — institutional lock-in, democratic alliance
- [[Realism]] — alliance for power aggregation, US instrument
- [[Constructivism]] — transatlantic identity, liberal community
- [[Security Dilemma]] — NATO expansion as trigger
- [[Deterrence]] — collective deterrence guarantee

## Key Cases
- [[Ukraine War]] — NATO resurgence, Finland/Sweden accession
- [[Kosovo War]] — first out-of-area offensive operation
- [[Cold War]] — founding mission

## Key Questions
1. Why has NATO survived the Cold War?
2. Did NATO expansion cause the Ukraine war?
3. Is NATO a defensive alliance or an offensive instrument?
4. Can NATO survive without US leadership?
