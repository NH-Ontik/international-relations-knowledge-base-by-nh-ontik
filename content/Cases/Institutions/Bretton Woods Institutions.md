# Bretton Woods Institutions (IMF, World Bank, GATT/WTO)

## Overview
The institutional backbone of the post-1945 liberal economic order. Central to debates on development, global inequality, conditionality, and neoliberal hegemony.

## Key Themes
- Liberal institutionalist success story
- Structural adjustment critiques
- Washington Consensus vs alternatives
- Western hegemony through economics
- Reform demands from Global South and BRICS
- Trade liberalisation vs protectionism

## Related Theories
- [[International Organizations]] — economic IOs as regime pillars
- [[Liberalism]] — institutions enable cooperation, reduce transaction costs
- [[Marxism]] — institutions of global capitalism, perpetuate inequality
- [[International Development]] — development models (SAPs criticised, MDGs/SDGs)
- [[Hegemony]] — US hegemony embedded in institutional architecture
- [[Postcolonialism]] — Global South experiences Bretton Woods as imposed order

## Key Concepts
- IMF: balance of payments support, conditionality
- World Bank: development lending, structural adjustment
- GATT→WTO: trade liberalisation, dispute settlement mechanism

## Key Questions
1. Do Bretton Woods institutions help or harm development?
2. Are they Western instruments or genuinely multilateral?
3. Can they reform fast enough to stay relevant?
4. What do BRICS/AIIB parallel institutions mean for the order?
