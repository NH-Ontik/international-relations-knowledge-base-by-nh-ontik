# Imperialism

A policy of extending a state's power through territorial acquisition or political and economic control over other regions. In IR, imperialism is distinct from colonialism in that it can operate through indirect domination — economic pressure, debt dependency, or military coercion — without formal settlement. [[Vladimir Lenin|Lenin]]'s *Imperialism, the Highest Stage of Capitalism* (1917) argued that capitalist states are compelled to seek foreign markets and investment outlets as domestic profits decline, making interimperialist rivalry and war inevitable. Hobson (1902) preceded Lenin in linking imperialism to domestic underconsumption and finance capital. Contemporary scholarship examines **neocolonialism** (economic dependency without direct rule), **cultural imperialism**, and **digital imperialism**. The [[Scramble for Africa]] (1884–1914) remains the paradigmatic case of formal imperial expansion, while post-1945 US and Soviet spheres of influence represent informal imperial systems.
