# Eurocentrism

The tendency to interpret world history, politics, and society through European categories, experiences, and standards presented as universal. In IR, Eurocentrism manifests in theories derived from Westphalian sovereignty (1648), the nation-state as the default unit of analysis, and historical periodisations that centre European events (e.g., the Peace of Westphalia, the Congress of Vienna, the Cold War). [[Postcolonialism|Postcolonial]] scholars argue that mainstream IR — realism, liberalism, even much of Marxism — assumes that Europe's historical trajectory is the template for all societies, marginalising non-European experiences, agency, and knowledge systems. The critique is that IR's core concepts — anarchy, sovereignty, the state — emerge from European history and are treated as universal when they may not fit the [[Global South]].
