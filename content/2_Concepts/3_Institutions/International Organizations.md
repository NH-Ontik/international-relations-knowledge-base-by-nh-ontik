# International Organizations

## Definition
Formal, continuous structures established by agreement between members (usually states) to pursue common purposes. Two types: **IGOs** (Intergovernmental — UN, EU, NATO) and **INGOs** (International Non-Governmental — Amnesty, Red Cross, MSF).

## Theoretical Perspectives on IOs
- **Realism**: IOs are instruments of powerful states — they do what the US/EU want; have no independent power
- **Institutionalism**: IOs reduce transaction costs, provide information, monitor compliance — they enable cooperation that would not otherwise exist
- **Constructivism**: IOs are norm entrepreneurs — they create ideas and spread norms (e.g., truth commissions, gender mainstreaming, anti-landmine campaign)
- **Marxism**: IOs manage capitalism on a global scale — the WTO implements neoliberal globalisation
- **Postcolonialism**: IOs reflect 1945 power structures — Security Council permanent 5, IMF weighted voting

## Key Debates
- **Autonomy vs Instrument** — Do IOs have independent power, or are they tools of states?
- **IO effectiveness** — Do they matter for outcomes? (Peacekeeping reduces conflict length; WTO agreements increase trade; UN sanctions work about 30% of the time)
- **Democratic deficit** — IOs make decisions that affect billions but have no direct democratic accountability
- **Bureaucratic dysfunction** — UN bureaucracy is slow, bloated, politicised
- **Reform** — UN Security Council permanent membership unchanged since 1945 (UK, FR, China, US, RU)
