# BSS (Hons) International Relations — Degree Roadmap

## Year 2 — Building Analytical Depth

### Semester 1
- IR theory deep-dive (learn theoretical frameworks deeply)
- Regional studies introduction
- Core concepts anchoring in each theory

### Semester 2
- Linking theory to case studies
- Developing critical analysis skills
- Beginning to identify potential thesis areas

**Goals for Year 2:**
- [ ] Solidify understanding of all 6 major IR theories
- [ ] Read at least 3 full scholarly books relevant to IR
- [ ] Develop structured note-taking habit (Obsidian vault)
- [ ] Start building essay-writing skills with comparative analysis

## Year 3 — Specialisation and Methods

### Key Areas to Develop
- Research methods (quantitative, qualitative, mixed)
- Regional specialisation
- Thematic focus (security / political economy / human rights / etc.)
- Critical theories (beyond mainstream)

**Goals for Year 3:**
- [ ] Choose thesis direction
- [ ] Complete research methods training
- [ ] Develop expertise in at least one region and one thematic area

## Year 4 — Thesis & Synthesis

### Thesis Planning Timeline
- **Year 3-end:** Topic identified, supervisor confirmed
- **Semester 1:** Literature review, methodology, proposal
- **Christmas break:** Data/fieldwork/archive research
- **Semester 2:** Writing, revisions, submission
